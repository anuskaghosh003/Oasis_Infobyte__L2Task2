Who is Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India - died July 27, 2015, Shillong), Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007.Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Had Became the 11th President of India on 25th July 2002.

He is a man of vision, who is always full of ideas aimed at the development of the country.

Education

He completed its education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram. He graduated in physics 1954 from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. He moved to Madras in 1955 to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.

His Awards

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam won many awards, both from the Indian government and from the international community. His most notable awards were the Padma Vibhushan, won in 1990, and the Bharat Ratna won in 1997, for his contributions to science and engineering and service to the government.
